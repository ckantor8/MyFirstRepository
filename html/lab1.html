An Analysis of The Opioid Crisis

With a Focus on Geographic Distribution

By Cody Kantor

One of the most pressing issues in America today is the ever-worsening opioid epidemic. Despite the overall national opioid prescribing rate falling in 2016 to the lowest it had been 10 years, the frequency of opioid-related overdoses continue to climb. Though, as you can see from this geographic map of opioid deaths, they are not equally distributed throughout the nation. So in order to further investigate this crisis and what causes it to be more prevalent in particular regions, I plan to compile data on the next page.
